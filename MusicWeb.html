What is music?

Music is a term that covers an indefinite meaning. It represents any sound, whether its the clinging of metal pots or the melodious sound of Beethoven playing the piano. There is no right kind of music. But what distinguishes the different sounds?

On a piano, there are 12 keys.

Each key signifies a different tune. But there are notes inbetween these keys, which are usually known as out of tune notes.

All keys have a major and two minor scales. The minor scales are classified as either harmonic minor scales or melodic minor scales.

Instruments

There are many types of instruments. All instruments are divided into four categories: Woodwinds, Percussion, Strings, and Brass. In each category, there are families of instruments.

Woodwinds

There are thousands of woodwind instruments. They known as reed and flute instruments. The most common reed instruments are the B♭ Clarinet, the Bass Clarinet, the Contre Alto Clarinet, the Alto Saxophone, the Tenor Saxophone, the Baritone Saxophone, the Oboe, the Basson, and the Contre Bassoon. Some common flutes are the C Flutes, the Alto Flutes, and the Piccolo.

Percussion

Percussion instruments are known for being able to bang on. They are classified into two sections; Battery Percussion, and Mallet Percussion. Some common battery percussion instruments are the Snare Drum, the Bass Drum, the Toms, the Symbals, and the Gongs. Some common mallet percussion instruments are the Marimba, the Chimes, the Bells, and the Timpanis.

String

String Instruments are known because those are the only instruments played with strings. Some common string instruments are the Piano, the Violin, the Viola, the Cello, and the String Bass.

Brass

Brass instruments are usually made of brass. That is why they are called brass instruments. Some common brass instruments are the Trumpet, the French Horn, the Euphonium, the Trombone, and the Tuba.
